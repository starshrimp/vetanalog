The Problem

A critical distraction
at the worst moment

Every five minutes during surgery, a vet anaesthesiologist must stop, look away from the patient, and manually record vitals on paper. Then spend hours after surgery transcribing those notes into digital systems.

✍️

Vitals recorded by hand every 5 minutes

Legally required manual documentation forces eyes off the patient at critical moments.

⚠️

Manual charting = critical distraction

Attention split between patient safety and paperwork increases risk during high-stakes procedures.

🕐

Hours lost post-op to transcription

Tired vets manually transfer paper notes into digital systems — data gets lost, burnout sets in.

4,650 anaesthesias per year at Tierspital Zürich
130k manual records written by hand annually
85% reduction with VetAnaLog
The Solution

From bedside to report —
automatically

1

Automatic monitor integration

Vitals captured directly from anaesthesia monitors. No manual entry, no missed data points.

2

Two-tap event logging

Log medications, events, and comments in seconds. Eyes stay on the patient — not the screen.

3

AI-generated anaesthesia report

At the end of surgery, our LLM drafts a complete structured report. Review, sign, export.

4

Structured data storage

Every procedure stored as research-ready data — the foundation for predictive AI.
