The Problem

Every five minutes.
Pen to paper. Mid-surgery.

Veterinary anaesthesiologists are legally required to manually record vitals every five minutes during surgery — a critical distraction at the worst possible moment. When surgery ends, hours of transcription follow.

📋

Dangerous Distraction

Every five minutes, attention is pulled from a high-risk patient to fill in a paper chart by hand. For a 500 kg horse under general anaesthesia, this is not acceptable.

⏱️

Hours of Transcription

After surgery ends, vets face hours of manually transferring handwritten notes into digital systems. Data gets lost, errors creep in, and professionals burn out.

📉

Data Never Captured

Handwritten records are fragmented and unstructured. The rich procedural data that could predict complications and improve outcomes is simply discarded.

⚠️

Legal Risk

Incomplete or inaccurate anaesthesia documentation creates serious legal exposure. Paper charts are error-prone and hard to audit — the status quo is untenable.

How It Works

From monitor to report — automatically.

VetAnaLog integrates directly into the surgical workflow without adding complexity. Less friction. More focus. Better outcomes.

1

Connect to the Monitor

VetAnaLog connects directly to the anaesthesia monitor via the GE Healthcare integration. All vitals stream in live — heart rate, SpO₂, ETCO₂, blood pressure, temperature.

2

Log Interventions in Two Taps

Medications, fluids, and observations are recorded with minimal interaction. The interface is designed for gloved hands and split-second attention.

3

AI Drafts the Report

When surgery ends, the LLM synthesises everything into a complete, structured anaesthesia report in seconds. The vet reviews, signs, and exports.

4

Data Contributes to the Network

With consent, anonymised surgical data joins the shared research network — building the world's first high-fidelity veterinary anaesthesia dataset.
